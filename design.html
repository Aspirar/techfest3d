Task

Each team has to make a working model of a product using the electronic kit provided on the spot.

Pre-Requirements

Every team should have a basic knowledge of arduino and microprocessor.

Rules

  • Each team can have a maximum of 2 members.
  • Teams will get their kits based on chit system and no exchange of kit either with other teams or with the event organizers can be made.
  • Each team must have to use the arduino/any other microcontroller board provided while building the working model.
  • All teams have to check if their components are properly working within first 5 minutes after the kit is provided, afterwards no excuses will be listened.
  • There will be two rounds, time limit for each will be 30 minutes.
  • After 1st round judges will decide which team has qualified for the 2nd round based on the performance of 1st round.
  • Teams registering for the event will be allowed to enter the event venue upto 15 minutes late after event commences.
  • Each team has to write a note of their working model point wise in this format (maximum in two or three lines)
    A. Product name
    B. What it does
    C. relevance in daily life
    D. kit used with name
  • In case of any conflict judges decision will be final.

General Rules

  • Teams should bring their own laptops to do the coding part.
  • Coordination committee has the right to add or update rules.

Winning Criteria

  • The working model should be more relevant to use in day to day life (The relevance will be decided by the judges).
  • Based on using fraction of items provided in the kit.
  • Coding complexity (will be decide by the judges).
